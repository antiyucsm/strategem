The Nyala Print

New Sudanese pound banknotes in the 500 and 1,000 denominations, bearing the signature of pre-war central bank governor Hussein Yahia Jangoul and backdated to May 2022, have been printed and distributed across most of the five Darfur states and parts of Kordofan over the past ten days. Civil servants and Rapid Support Forces fighters have been paid in them. The notes circulate under the operational authority of the “Transitional Currency Council” established by Tasis Prime Minister Mohamed Hassan al-Taayishi’s May 11 decree and chaired by Jangoul since his May 21 appointment as head of a new RSF-aligned central bank seated in Nyala. They arrive against the backdrop of a Friday RSF drone strike on a funeral procession and the airport district at El-Obeid in Kordofan that killed twenty-two civilians, and against the Port Sudan government’s standing demonetisation programme that has already collapsed the pound from below six hundred to over five thousand to the US dollar. The Stratagem reads the Nyala Print as the move at which Sudan’s partition stops being a battlefield map and becomes a balance-sheet fact — a parallel state issues legal tender, pays salaries in it, and embeds itself in the cohort of civil servants, soldiers, and merchants who now depend on the new currency to eat. Mearsheimer on the sovereignty produced when monopoly of violence and monopoly of money compound; Greene’s eleventh law on dependence as the durable instrument; Munger on the inversion that asks what cannot be undone; Lee Kuan Yew on the cadre that holds a salary owing the regime that pays it.

From the Desk of Statecraft·Filed Nyala, Port Sudan & Abu Dhabi·27 June 2026

Nyala —The news is not the funeral strike at El-Obeid, which is the kind of atrocity the war has produced on a monthly cycle for three years. The news is also not the Tasis decree that established a parallel central bank, which was a piece of political theatre on a single day in May. The news is what happens when those two facts compound across the ten days between them: a fighter is paid, in cash, on payday, in a new banknote, by a state that until this month was a militia. The economic register has overtaken the kinetic register. A war fought in trucks and drones produces a politics of who controls which town; a war that produces its own currency produces a politics of who controls which payroll. The Sudanese pound that circulates west of the White Nile is not the Sudanese pound that circulates east of it. The cohort that handles the western pound is institutionally embedded in the cohort that prints it. That is the architectural fact the past ten days have installed.

Begin with Mearsheimer. The realist tradition reserves a sharp definition for sovereignty: the entity that monopolises the use of force inside a territory and is recognised as doing so by the population that lives there. The recognition condition is the loose end — Weber’s formulation makes it the load-bearing variable, and twentieth-century international law made it a function of external recognition by other states. But the operational version of recognition is not a UN vote; it is whether the population accepts the currency the entity issues, pays taxes to the entity, and pays its soldiers in coin the population will accept. The RSF held the violence monopoly across most of Darfur and parts of Kordofan from late 2024. What the Nyala Print adds is the second monopoly. A government is recognised by the cohort that takes its money. The civil servant in Nyala who collects a salary in Jangoul-signed pounds and uses them to buy bread at the market has, by the act, recognised the parallel state. The merchant who accepts the notes has converted the recognition into commerce. The currency does what the kinetic campaign could not: it produces the daily, mundane, irreversible micro-acts of acquiescence that twentieth-century political science calls legitimacy and the realist tradition calls the second monopoly.

Then read Greene’s eleventh law. Learn to keep people dependent on you. The discipline is not the cultivation of loyalty — loyalty is volatile, expensive, and reversible — but the engineering of conditions inside which the cohort cannot exit without absorbing a larger cost than the cost of staying. The RSF is paying the Darfur civil-service cohort, the Tasis bureaucracy, and its own fighters in notes that have no legal validity east of the Sahel-Nile divide. The recipients cannot bank them in Khartoum, cannot remit them to family in Port Sudan, cannot spend them in any market the SAF reaches. The exit cost has been engineered upward: leaving the parallel-state cohort means abandoning the salary that feeds the family. The standing cohort is no longer being recruited by ideology or coerced by force; it is being held by the dependence that the currency produces. La Rochefoucauld’s discipline that self-interest speaks every language, and even the language of virtue reads the framing the Tasis architecture chose: “monetary stabilisation, banking regulation, transitional currency council” is the vocabulary of central banking, not paramilitary insurgency. The vocabulary is the second instrument; the salary is the first.

What the print installs.

Munger’s inversion — do not ask what you want; ask what you cannot afford to undo — reads the move at its strategic register. The standing African Union and IGAD mediation architecture has framed the conflict as resolvable by ceasefire-plus-political-process: a cessation of hostilities, a transitional executive that bridges SAF and RSF cohorts, an internationally supervised reconstruction. That framework rested on a sovereign entity that could be partitioned in personnel without being partitioned in institution — one central bank, one currency, one tax collector, with the seats around the table contested. The Nyala Print retires that frame. There are now two central banks, two currencies, two civil services drawing from two payrolls. Reunification is no longer a political negotiation; it is a monetary integration. The Bosnian and Cypriot precedents are the operational manuals: once two currencies circulate in two zones for eighteen months, the cost of monetary reunification exceeds the political will of either cohort to absorb, and the de facto partition becomes the equilibrium even where no de jure recognition exists. The RSF has not declared independence; it has done something harder to undo — it has issued a payroll. Lee Kuan Yew’s discipline that the cadre that holds a salary owes the regime that pays it reads the cumulative effect: every payday compounds the dependence, and every compounding payday raises the integration cost the mediation cohort would have to absorb.

What the calendar tests.

Three variables across the next ninety days will reveal whether the print holds. First, the cross-rate between the Nyala pound and the Port Sudan pound on the black market: a stable or appreciating Nyala rate signals that the RSF cohort has secured enough commodity-and-gold backing to defend the currency, which in turn signals that the UAE supply chain through the Adré-and-Sirba border crossings is intact. A collapsing Nyala rate signals the opposite. Second, the merchant-acceptance arc across the major Darfur and Kordofan markets: if the notes are still being accepted at par by traders ninety days from circulation, the legitimacy instrument has compounded; if they are being discounted or refused, the cohort has begun to defect. Third, the response architecture of the standing African Union, IGAD, Tahrir-and-Cairo, and US-Treasury OFAC cohorts: the standing question is whether the parallel currency triggers a designation cycle that imposes secondary-sanction risk on the merchant cohort handling the notes, or whether the international cohort treats the currency as a fait accompli and pivots toward de facto recognition of the partition. The modal path across the window is the second: the standing cohort has revealed an exhausted bandwidth for Sudan across two-and-a-half years, the standing UAE-and-Egyptian-and-Saudi mediation architecture has been frustrated by the SAF-RSF refusal cycle, and an unrecognised partition that holds is the equilibrium toward which the international cohort drifts when active management has failed. Confucius on the rectification of names reads the broader register: what the African Union still calls “the war in Sudan” will be renamed across the standing cycle as “the two Sudans,” and the renaming is the institutional ratification of what the currency has already produced.

Reading Note

The lenses used.

What’s driving this: The coordination layer carries the weight here. What has been built in Nyala over the past six weeks is a coordinated apparatus of state — a decree, a council, an appointed governor, a captured branch network, a payroll, a backing flow of gold-export revenue through the UAE refining cohort — that converts a militia’s violence monopoly into the second monopoly that twentieth-century political science calls legitimacy. The print embeds the recipient cohort in the parallel state through the most boring instrument in the catalogue: the salary. The structural layer underneath is the SAF’s own demonetisation programme: replacing the pre-war pound east of the Nile opened the gap inside which the unswapped Jangoul-signed stock could be deployed as a parallel currency west of it. The combination is the architectural fact — partition stops being a battlefield outcome and becomes a payroll calendar that compounds every month it runs.

What’s not driving this: A reading that frames the parallel currency as financial improvisation or RSF brand-building misses the institutional careful work behind it. The decree predates the print by six weeks, the governor predates the notes by one, the gold backing is on three years of accumulated supply chain, and the timing is sequenced against the SAF demonetisation rather than independent of it. This is not a militia faking sovereignty; it is a parallel state monetising the recognition cycle.

What would break this reading:

  • The Nyala-pound cross-rate against the Port Sudan pound collapses by more than fifty per cent inside ninety days — the “gold-backed currency holds par” reading was wrong; the cohort that backs the currency cannot defend it, and the dependence instrument has been broken at the cross-rate rather than at the salary.
  • The standing UAE financing-and-supply architecture is publicly suspended or visibly degraded inside six months — the “backer cohort is durable” reading was wrong; the parallel-state apparatus runs on a thinner financial base than the print presupposes.
  • The SAF retakes Nyala or El Fasher inside twelve months and seizes the printing-and-distribution architecture — the “partition has become a balance-sheet fact” reading was wrong; the kinetic register has reasserted itself over the monetary register.
  • The US Treasury OFAC designates the parallel notes and the Dubai gold-refining cohort that backs them, and the merchant cohort across Darfur discounts or refuses the notes inside six months — the “international cohort drifts toward de facto recognition” reading was wrong; secondary-sanction pressure has overwhelmed the dependence instrument.

From the Desk of Capital·Filed New York & London·27 June 2026

New York —The year-end target on a sell-side equity-strategy note is the part of the document the news wire copies; the flash-crash warning is the part that gets buried in the third paragraph of the morning summary. Friday’s JPMorgan note crossed the wires as “7,800” for the same reason every other forecast upgrade does — the headline number is the unit of cohort comparison the buy-side desks compete on. But the note that Dubravko Lakos-Bujas’s desk actually wrote is not a forecast at all. It is a two-sided positioning prescription, packaged inside the cohort vocabulary of bull targets, that gives one product to the cohort that wants permission to stay long and a different product to the cohort that wants permission to hedge. The headline answers the question the marketing department asked. The third paragraph answers the question the risk department asked. The institution is not predicting where the index will close; it is selling protection on its own franchise across the path the index will take.

Begin with Munger. The discipline that selective honesty is the durable instrument because the cohort that catches you in a lie remembers, and the cohort that catches you in a calibrated truth becomes a customer reads the structure of the note at its institutional register. The standing post-2008 sell-side equity-strategy desk has been the cohort most aggressively pruned by the regulatory cycle: MiFID-II compelled unbundled research pricing, the standing best-execution architecture compressed the soft-dollar revenue base, and the standing passive-index cycle reduced active-management demand for the desk’s output. What remains is a cohort whose institutional purpose is to be cited — in earnings calls, in client meetings, in the Bloomberg consensus — and whose franchise risk is to be falsified. A forecast that is high is good for the citation cycle if the index runs; a forecast that proves wrong but was paired with a flash-crash warning lets the desk argue that the cohort that read past the third paragraph was correctly hedged. Lakos-Bujas’s desk is not contradicting itself. It is constructing an institutional position that wins on both legs of the path: 7,800 is the headline that wins on the way up, and the long-quality short-low-quality factor hedge is the position that wins on the way down. Either tape, the desk has a citable record.

Then read Greene’s thirteenth law. When asking for help, appeal to people’s self-interest, never to their mercy or gratitude. The discipline reads the audience design of the note at its register. The cohort that reads only the 7,800 target is the long-only fund whose marketing material needs an authoritative bull case to justify the standing equity allocation; the cohort that reads only the flash-crash warning is the macro hedge fund whose risk committee needs an authoritative caution to justify the long-vol or factor-pair position. Each cohort gets the product it needed before it opened the note, and each is moved toward the bank’s research-and-execution franchise as a paying customer rather than as a conscience-driven supporter. La Rochefoucauld’s observation that self-interest speaks every language, and even the language of virtue reads the framing the desk chose: the “blue sky scenario” vocabulary is the language of optimism, and the “flash crash” vocabulary is the language of fiduciary caution. The two languages cohabit inside the same paragraph because they address two cohorts inside the same client base. The note is a piece of multi-audience communication theatre dressed as a single-cohort forecast.

What the hedge installs.

Dalio’s Big-Cycle framework reads the timing. The bull target is the institutional accommodation to the cohort that needs the headline; the flash-crash warning is the institutional protection of the franchise across the path. Munger’s inversion — do not ask where the index will close; ask what the desk needs to be true to defend the franchise on either tape — reads the substance: the 7,800 print is the bull case the index could plausibly hit, the 8,900 print is the blue-sky scenario the bull-case cohort wants to see in writing, and the flash-crash warning is the rear-guard hedge the franchise needs against the crowded-momentum tail. The note is honest about all three; it is just honest in a way that addresses three different cohorts.

What the calendar tests.

Three variables across the next two quarters will reveal whether the note’s positioning prescription holds. First, the breadth of the rally: if the equal-weight S&P 500 narrows its underperformance against the cap-weight index and the index advance continues to be driven by the standing AI-and-hyperscaler cohort alone, the flash-crash warning is the load-bearing instrument; if breadth broadens and small-and-mid cap participation increases, the bull case is operationally installed and the warning becomes vestigial. Third, the cohort positioning data: if the prime-broker sub-cohort data shows continued increases in the standing long-momentum exposure across the hedge-fund cohort, the “extreme crowding” warning is being institutionally ratified rather than dismissed, and the flash-crash tail risk compounds. The modal path across the window is that the index drifts toward the 7,600-to-7,800 zone on continued AI-earnings momentum, the breadth remains narrow, the cohort hedge underperforms in absolute terms but compounds the franchise credibility across the September-October-November cycle, and the flash-crash warning becomes the desk’s standing institutional discipline for the next twelve months. Confucius on the rectification of names reads the broader register: what the cohort still calls “the bull case” is a piece of cohort-marketing instrument design, and what the cohort still calls “the flash-crash warning” is the franchise rear-guard. The cohort that reads both registers correctly does not get an answer; it gets a positioning prescription.

Law of the Day · No. 13

Appeal to self-interest, never to mercy or gratitude.

Greene’s thirteenth law reads as the discipline of the principal who has understood the constituent he is moving. When asking a cohort to do what you need it to do, the request that lands is the one phrased in the cohort’s own ledger. Gratitude is the most volatile currency in human affairs — received once, it pays no compound interest; reminded of, it converts into resentment; relied on, it dissolves at the moment a counter-offer arrives with a better number. Mercy is the second-most volatile — granted, it puts the principal who granted it in the position of the supplicant’s permanent debtor; expected, it is the strongest signal the cohort can read that the principal has nothing else to offer. Self-interest is the durable instrument. Saturday’s three stories share the same signature: a principal who structured the architecture so the cohort needed for the move is moved by its own ledger rather than by appeal to ours.

Today’s reading: the Nyala Print, the Blue Sky Hedge, the Wolfsburg Reset. Three operations across three registers, each a different application of the same discipline. In Sudan, the RSF cohort did not ask Darfuri civil servants to switch sides; it issued a banknote and made the salary depend on the new state — the cohort acts on its own self-interest the moment it accepts the first pay packet. In New York, JPMorgan did not ask its long-only clients to trust the bull case and its hedge-fund clients to trust the flash-crash warning; it published both inside one note and let each cohort identify its own product. In Wolfsburg, the Vorstand did not ask the IG Metall cohort to accept the four-plant list as a sacrifice for the German Modell; it presented the list as a fait accompli inside a margin-recovery vocabulary that addressed only the equity-cohort self-interest.

  • The Nyala Print is the law at the state-formation register — the RSF cohort built a salary architecture that recruits the recipient through self-interest, not through ideology or coercion, and the dependence compounds across every payday.
  • The Blue Sky Hedge is the law at the institutional-communication register — the JPMorgan note addressed two cohorts inside one paragraph by giving each the product its self-interest required, and the franchise wins on either tape.
  • The Wolfsburg Reset is the law at the corporate-governance register — the Vorstand addressed the equity-cohort self-interest by retiring the bargain it had previously made with the labour-cohort self-interest, and the political settlement that depended on the prior bargain will now rebalance.
From the Classics

Greene & La Rochefoucauld.

If you need to turn to an ally for help, do not bother to remind him of your past assistance and good deeds. He will find a way to ignore you. Instead, uncover something in your request, or in your alliance with him, that will benefit him, and emphasise it out of all proportion. He will respond enthusiastically when he sees something to be gained for himself. Self-interest is the lever that moves people; once you know how to play on it you have a kind of magic wand. Never appeal to gratitude; in the world of power, you cannot rely on it. The clear-eyed asker reframes the request in the cohort’s own ledger and asks the cohort to act on the ledger’s arithmetic, not on the residue of a relationship the cohort would prefer to forget.

Self-interest speaks every language, and even the language of virtue. The cohort that frames its hardest move in the vocabulary of moral improvement, of necessary modernisation, of long-run sustainability, has not abandoned the calculation; it has translated the calculation into the vocabulary the audience will accept. The translator is not the deceiver; the translator is the principal who has understood that the audience must hear the appeal in the language its self-interest prefers. The wise principal studies which vocabulary the cohort uses to describe its own self-interest and frames the move in that vocabulary. The cohort accepts the move not because the vocabulary is honest but because the vocabulary is its own.

The Practitioner’s Note

Move the cohort with its own ledger.

Each of Saturday’s stories turns on the same instrument: the principal who needed the cohort’s acquiescence structured the architecture so the cohort acts on its own self-interest, not on a residual loyalty or moral appeal. The RSF cohort did not negotiate with Darfuri civil servants for political loyalty; it printed a banknote, paid them in it, and the dependence accumulated across every payday at the cadence the cohort itself controls. JPMorgan’s strategist desk did not ask its competing client cohorts to choose between the bull case and the cautionary case; it gave each cohort the product its own desk’s self-interest required, and the institution’s franchise wins on either tape because each cohort was sold what it came to buy. Volkswagen’s Vorstand did not ask the IG Metall cohort to trade the Modell’s standing settlement for a margin-recovery programme; it presented the four-plant list as a fait accompli, addressed only the equity-cohort self-interest, and forced the labour-cohort to respond inside the cohort’s own constrained framework. The discipline is not the willingness to manipulate; it is the design of the architecture inside which each cohort moves of its own accord.

In your own affairs: the law cuts at three registers. At the professional register, the cohort whose help you need — a peer’s endorsement of your project, a manager’s release of a resource, a counterparty’s acceptance of a clause — will move only if you have understood the cohort’s own ledger. The appeal to past favours, to your shared institutional history, or to the project’s broader importance is the language of gratitude; it is read by the cohort as the absence of a more substantive appeal. The appeal to the cohort’s upside in your move — what they gain, what they avoid, what they look like to their own cohort — is the language of self-interest; it is read by the cohort as the language of decision-relevant information. At the personal register, the change you want a member of your cohort to absorb — a household discipline, a relational rhythm, a financial decision — lands at the cadence at which you have framed it in their ledger, not in yours. The reframing is not the manipulation; it is the translation. At the institutional register, the cohort that asks its constituents to act on gratitude or duty is the cohort that has not understood that gratitude and duty are residual instruments. The cohort that designs the architecture so the constituents act on their own self-interest is the cohort that endures across cycles.

The hard question: the law has a second edge. The principal who plays only on self-interest forfeits the cohort the principal will need at the moment self-interest cuts the other way. The RSF’s currency-and-salary architecture works until the cross-rate collapses and the dependence becomes a trap the cohort has no incentive to remain inside. JPMorgan’s two-product note works until one tape definitively prevails and the cohort that bought the wrong product asks why the franchise sold it. The Vorstand’s margin-recovery posture works until the labour-cohort organises its own self-interest against the cohort that retired the standing compact. Self-interest is the lever; it is not the foundation. The principal who builds only on the lever loses the foundation when the cohort’s self-interest realigns. Before the execution, ask: have I addressed the cohort’s self-interest as the operative instrument, and have I preserved enough of the residual foundation — reputation, residual loyalty, shared institutional history — that I can call on it when self-interest cuts the other way? The lever and the foundation are not the same; the principal who needs only the lever today will need the foundation at the cycle that lever cannot reach.
