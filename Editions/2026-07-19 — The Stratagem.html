The Port Moresby Switch

Papua New Guinea’s Foreign Minister Justin Tkatchenko announced on Thursday 16 July that Taiwan’s economic and trade representative office in Port Moresby would be closed. Beijing praised the move within hours; Taipei rejected it as unilateral; the United States declared itself “deeply concerned”; and Prime Minister James Marape’s cabinet is now visibly split on whether the foreign minister spoke for the government at all. The closure is the specific completion of a fifteen-year Beijing campaign in which Taipei’s roster of diplomatic and quasi-diplomatic partners has shrunk from twenty-three to twelve official capitals plus a diminishing number of trade offices, one Pacific micro-state at a time, using development finance and single-official inducement rather than force. Read as a Port Moresby cabinet dispute the switch is a diplomatic curiosity. Read as coordinated power it is the specific move by which the People’s Republic converts Pacific-island debt exposure and single-minister capture into the systematic isolation of the entity Xi Jinping named in the 2049-centenary speech as the “final territorial task.”

The sequence, first.

Read the announcement itself carefully. Tkatchenko’s statement was made on Thursday morning Port Moresby time, published on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade website inside two hours, and welcomed at Beijing’s regular Ministry of Foreign Affairs press briefing by 4 p.m. Beijing time. The wording — that Chinese Taipei’s physical presence would no longer be “recognised or required” — adopts the specific phrasing of the 1971 UN General Assembly Resolution 2758 that Beijing has been quietly enforcing as customary international law since Wang Yi’s 2023 Munich address. By late afternoon Prime Minister James Marape’s office in Waigani was fielding calls from Taipei, Canberra and Washington and telling all three the same thing: the foreign minister had not cleared the announcement with cabinet. Taipei’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs issued a statement at 21:00 local rejecting the closure as a “unilateral act.” Vice President Hsiao Bi-khim, in a specifically pointed Friday-morning briefing, said the pattern of PNG’s decision was “the pattern of Chinese suppression everywhere.” The State Department’s Bureau of East Asian and Pacific Affairs issued its “deeply concerned” statement on Friday evening Washington time. By Saturday the Marape government had made no clarification — the classic pattern of a Pacific-island executive that has been captured but does not wish the capture named — and the office in Port Moresby was still physically operating, its status the specific question the coming week will resolve.

Sun Tzu on winning without fighting.

Sun Tzu’s third chapter opens with the discipline the Communist Party has organised the Taiwan question around since Deng: the acme of skill is to subdue the enemy without fighting. The chapter’s specific instruction is that the great general breaks the enemy’s alliances before the enemy’s army. Beijing’s Taiwan doctrine has read the chapter to the letter: not an amphibious landing on the Fujian coast, not a naval blockade of the Strait, but a fifteen-year attritional campaign against Taipei’s diplomatic partners, its unofficial representative offices, its trade agreements, its participation in every UN specialised agency from ICAO to Interpol. Taiwan held twenty-three official diplomatic partners in 2016; today it holds twelve. Solomon Islands switched in September 2019, Kiribati four days later, Nicaragua in December 2021, Honduras in March 2023, Nauru in January 2024. PNG’s move on 16 July is not a rupture; it is the ratcheting-forward of a diplomatic isolation strategy the Party executes at the pace the outside world will tolerate. The specific power of Sun Tzu’s discipline is that every peeled partner reduces the number of capitals that would speak against Taipei at the UN Security Council on the day the amphibious question becomes concrete, and every day of that isolation is a day of coordinated strategic depth Beijing purchases below the threshold at which the American security guarantee is triggered. The West reads each Pacific switch as a curiosity; the Party reads them as the specific instruments of the Sun Tzu discipline.

Greene’s Law 42 on the shepherd.

Robert Greene’s forty-second Law — strike the shepherd and the sheep will scatter — reads the individual-official capture pattern that has become Beijing’s operative Pacific method. The Port Moresby announcement was Tkatchenko’s, not Marape’s. The Solomon Islands 2019 switch was Prime Minister Manasseh Sogavare’s, executed against significant cabinet opposition and against Malaita province’s open resistance; a security agreement with the People’s Republic followed in April 2022. The Nauru 2024 switch was announced by President David Adeang three days after his election, and the reported price was the Nauru government’s outstanding obligations to a departing Australian mining venture. The Honduras 2023 switch was President Xiomara Castro’s within four weeks of a Beijing visit by her foreign minister. Greene’s discipline is that the operator who identifies the single-decision-maker inside an organisation, cultivates them privately, and asks them for one specific move that the organisation as a whole would refuse if consulted, captures the organisation without ever confronting it. The Party has organised its Pacific and Central American operations around this Law with a discipline the Western foreign services have refused to name because naming it concedes both the discipline and the pattern. The Marape cabinet’s Friday retreat — the private walk-back to Canberra, Washington and Taipei that the public statement has not walked back — is the specific tell.

Mearsheimer on the price of gravity wells.

John Mearsheimer’s offensive-realist reading of small-state behaviour is not the flattering one. Small states inside a regional hegemon’s gravity well do not choose their orientation; they are priced. The specific instrument of pricing is Chinese development finance: PNG’s public external debt to Chinese lenders was USD 590 million in 2010, USD 2.1 billion in 2019, and an estimated USD 3.4 billion at the end of 2025 — roughly a fifth of the country’s external debt stack, and inside the range at which the IMF’s debt-sustainability assessment turns from “moderate” to “high” risk of distress. Solomon Islands’ equivalent number ran from USD 30 million to USD 660 million across the same span. Kiribati received a Chinese-funded rehabilitation of the Kanton Island runway in 2022, at a value the government has never disclosed but that IMF technical assistance estimates at seven per cent of one year’s GDP. The Pacific-island economies are structurally short of infrastructure capital and structurally long of maritime real estate the People’s Republic values for the second-island-chain calculation. The specific realism-discipline is not that any individual switch was purchased; it is that the entire class of Pacific-island decisions runs inside a Chinese pricing envelope Australia and the United States entered too late and priced too low. The AUKUS Pillar-II Pacific-uplift announcement of March 2025 was the West’s belated recognition; the July 2026 PNG announcement is the continuing arithmetic.

The Solomon Islands 2019 rhyme.

The immediate operational rhyme is not the 1971 UN vote; it is Solomon Islands, September 2019. On 16 September 2019 Prime Minister Sogavare’s cabinet voted 27-0 to sever diplomatic recognition of Taiwan, ratifying a decision Sogavare had communicated to Beijing more than a month earlier. Kiribati followed inside seventy-two hours. Malaita province declared its opposition, the United States responded with a Pacific Deterrence Initiative that took two budget cycles to fund, and by April 2022 Sogavare had signed the security-cooperation agreement with the People’s Republic that the Solomon Islands cabinet had not approved. The three-year arc — recognition-switch, Western reaction of concern, security agreement, external partner locked in — is the specific rhyme the PNG closure telegraphs. The 2026 execution is subtler in one specific way: the Marape cabinet has not signed off, and the public statement can still be walked back in a way the Sogavare cabinet vote could not. It is heavier in another: PNG is Melanesia’s largest state, shares its only land border with Indonesia’s Papua provinces, hosts the Lombrum naval base that Australia and the United States upgraded in 2023 under the Comprehensive Strategic and Economic Partnership, and sits astride the second-island-chain calculation Beijing has organised the last decade of Pacific operations around.

The alternative case is a full Marape reversal by Wednesday, Tkatchenko’s resignation or dismissal, and the West briefly reads the campaign as blunted — but the Party has been running the campaign at the pace of below-threshold acquisition for fifteen years, and one reversed announcement does not reverse the arithmetic. The base case is more probable; the alternative case is louder but not structurally different; the specific variable is whether the Marape cabinet clears the Tkatchenko announcement inside seventy-two hours or drifts into next week.

The lenses used.

What’s driving this. Two layers do the work. Coordination: Beijing runs a fifteen-year campaign against Taipei’s diplomatic and quasi-diplomatic partners by identifying the single decision-maker inside each Pacific-island or Central American cabinet, cultivating them privately with development finance and elite courtship, and asking for one specific switch decision the cabinet as a whole would refuse if consulted. The specific mechanism is single-official capture at the point where personal incentive meets structural debt exposure. Structural: Pacific-island economies sit inside a Chinese pricing envelope the West entered too late. PNG’s public external debt to Chinese lenders ran USD 590 million in 2010, USD 2.1 billion in 2019, USD 3.4 billion at end-2025; the equivalent Solomon Islands number went from USD 30 million to USD 660 million. The structural short of infrastructure capital, the structural long of maritime real estate, and the specific IMF debt-distress threshold PNG has crossed together form the arithmetic inside which every individual Tkatchenko-style decision is priced.

What’s not driving this. Ideology is not driving this. PNG’s domestic politics have no meaningful pro-China constituency and no meaningful pro-Taiwan constituency; the switch is not being sold to a voter base. And military coercion is not driving this either; Beijing has been running the Taiwan-isolation campaign specifically below the threshold at which the US security guarantee is triggered, and the fifteen-year attrition arithmetic works precisely because it stays below that line.

  • Marape summons cabinet on Monday, publicly reverses the Tkatchenko announcement in full, dismisses or accepts the resignation of the foreign minister, and PNG announces no additional Chinese financing envelope in the following six months; the Sun Tzu below-threshold discipline is read as having overplayed its hand and Beijing loses a Pacific partner for the first time in the cycle.

  • A specific Pacific-island partner that had already switched (Kiribati, Solomon Islands or Nauru) publicly announces a rebalancing away from Chinese exclusivity in the next twelve months; the pattern the PNG announcement was read as extending begins to be reread as reversible.
  • The AUKUS Pillar-II Pacific-uplift envelope announced in March 2025 delivers a specifically visible infrastructure project (submarine cable, wharf modernisation, aviation upgrade) at Port Moresby, Rabaul or Lae inside eighteen months, and the pricing-envelope reading of PNG behaviour begins to be crossed by a Western pricing envelope at credible scale.

The Gemini Stall

Bloomberg reported Thursday morning that Google’s Gemini 3.5 Pro model — the flagship the enterprise API business, the search-generative-experience rollout, and the Waymo-adjacent robotics research have all been priced on — has missed its Friday 17 July internal launch target after already missing a June window and an earlier July date set following an architectural rebuild of the original model. Alphabet shares closed 4.4 per cent lower, USD 130 billion of market cap erased in a session, on the day TSMC beat guide and Netflix missed on revenue. Anthropic’s Claude Fable 5, released 9 June, currently holds a 68 per cent probability on the Polymarket “best AI model end of 2026” market; Google trades at 13. Read as one delay the miss is a schedule slip in a category where slips are normal. Read as coordinated repricing it is the specific moment the world’s largest advertising business, sitting on the largest proprietary compute cluster and the deepest internet dataset ever assembled, is forced to reveal that its execution ceiling at the frontier is lower than the two smaller labs sitting between it and the customer-stack margin.

The sequence, first.

The specific sequence matters. Gemini 3.5 Flash shipped 19 May at the Google I/O keynote with genuine capability at low cost and a 1M-token context window, and briefly led the LMSys leaderboard on efficiency metrics. Gemini 3.5 Pro — the frontier reasoning-and-coding sibling — was previewed at the same keynote for “end of June.” It missed. Google DeepMind executives, per Bloomberg’s Thursday sourcing, discovered structural failures during pre-release benchmarking in two specific capabilities: recursive tool-calling (the model’s ability to nest one function-call inside another for multi-step agentic tasks) and SVG generation (a capability the enterprise design-tool integration roadmap depends on). Rather than patch the model, DeepMind scrapped the training run in early June and initiated a full architectural rebuild targeted for a mid-July release. That release was itself missed on 17 July: the model still trails Claude Fable 5 on SWE-bench Verified, on ARC-AGI-2, on the Anthropic-authored WebArena, and on the OpenAI-authored MRCR-2 by margins the Chatbot Arena Elo scores confirm at 1420 for Fable 5 vs an unreleased 1370 for the current 3.5 Pro snapshot. Sundar Pichai has publicly retained a “before end of Q3” framing; the internal date the DeepMind side is now working to is a stopgap 3.4.5 Pro release in early August with the full 3.5 Pro slipping to a late-October window. The 4 per cent Thursday move priced the pattern rather than the specific missed date.

Munger on the missing disclosure.

Munger’s disclosure discipline is that the strongest signal in any earnings cycle is the disclosure the operator declines to make. Alphabet reports Q2 on 24 July. The specific missing disclosure the Bloomberg report has now flushed into the open is that Google’s frontier model position is worse than the search-plus-cloud aggregate the segment reporting shows. Google Cloud Platform grew 30 per cent last quarter with a 15 per cent operating margin; Vertex AI API revenue is inside the Cloud segment and not broken out; the Gemini-branded consumer product is inside Search and Advertising and also not broken out. The absence of specific enterprise-API revenue disclosure in the last three quarterly filings was the disclosure discipline’s tell. Invert: what fact would a specific enterprise-API revenue disclosure have made unavoidable? That Anthropic’s API revenue is now above USD 8 billion annualised per the Sacra July revision, that OpenAI’s is above USD 20 billion, that Google’s Vertex-AI API revenue is inside the USD 2-3 billion band, and that the specific customer accounts where the API revenue is booked (Cursor, Vercel, Perplexity, Character, and the Anthropic-native Claude Code product) are Google Cloud infrastructure customers whose model spend is not sitting on Gemini. The 4.4 per cent Thursday move is the market pricing that inversion for the first time.

Thompson on distribution monopoly vs product monopoly.

Ben Thompson’s aggregation-theory discipline distinguishes between the operator who has captured distribution and the operator who has the best product. Distribution is what Google has: two billion Chrome users, four billion Android devices, a Search box the whole open web routes through, YouTube’s daily 4-billion-hour watchlist, Google Workspace inside every enterprise. The frontier-model product race is a different question and Thompson’s discipline is that distribution monopolies are worth what they earn until a new value-chain layer forms, and when it does the distribution monopoly is worth what it earns inside the new layer. The AI frontier is that new layer. Google’s specific strategic problem is that its distribution advantages — TPU-versus-GPU compute cost, YouTube training data, search-query training data, Waymo and Maps embodied-AI data — are the assets its ordinary-course business models incentivise it to hold rather than deploy. Anthropic has no distribution and no proprietary data; it has a research-plus-safety culture, a pure-API business model, and a $200-plus-billion valuation the market has priced on the specific belief that the winner-at-the-frontier captures the enterprise API tier regardless of who owns distribution. The Thursday tape prices that belief on Google’s worst intraday trading day since the Bard demo of February 2023.

Greene’s Law 25 on re-creation.

Greene’s twenty-fifth Law — re-create yourself — reads the DeepMind organisational structure at the register the Alphabet ten-K refuses to. Demis Hassabis in 2023 was made head of the merged Google DeepMind organisation, absorbing Google Brain into the AlphaGo lineage; the Gemini series is the merged organisation’s flagship. The specific tension Greene’s Law names is that a research organisation whose intellectual identity was formed on games (AlphaGo, AlphaStar, AlphaZero), protein folding (AlphaFold), and mathematics (AlphaProof) has to re-create itself as a productisation organisation whose enterprise-API benchmark performance is measured weekly on the Chatbot Arena. The specific evidence of the re-creation failure is the 3.5 Pro architectural rebuild. When the pre-release benchmarks showed the model losing to Claude Fable 5 on WebArena and MRCR-2, the operational choice was between shipping-and-iterating (the OpenAI-Anthropic release cadence) and rebuilding-and-shipping-once (the Google-DeepMind research-organisation cadence). DeepMind chose the second and missed the window. Greene’s discipline is that the operator who cannot re-create their operating cadence to match the environment’s tempo loses the pattern the environment rewards.

The Xerox PARC rhyme.

The imperfect historical rhyme is not Bing versus Google 2005 (Google’s current position is closer to Microsoft’s of that era than the reverse); it is Xerox PARC 1973-1980. Xerox Palo Alto Research Center invented the graphical user interface, the mouse, laser printing, Ethernet, the object-oriented programming paradigm, and the Alto personal computer. Xerox as a corporation was in the copier business, and copiers made the money; the specific fact of PARC was that Xerox’s corporate incentives ran against productising PARC’s inventions because doing so would have cannibalised the copier line before it was ready to be cannibalised. Steve Jobs walked through PARC in December 1979, saw the future, and Apple productised it. Google’s copier is search advertising and its PARC is DeepMind. The specific 2026 tell is that when the frontier-model race demanded a research-plus-productisation cadence the entire corporation did not incentivise, DeepMind’s response was a research-organisation architectural rebuild rather than a productisation iteration. The rhyme is not that Google loses the way Xerox lost; Google’s cash generation is larger and its distribution assets are stickier. The rhyme is that a research organisation attached to the wrong corporate incentive structure produces the frontier and loses the productisation cycle to organisations with the correct one, and the missing enterprise-API revenue disclosure is the tell.
