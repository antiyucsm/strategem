The Litani Tender

A short joint statement from the Department of State on Friday confirmed that the three governments — the United States, the Republic of Lebanon and the State of Israel — have agreed to reconvene the political and security tracks the week of June 22, with the fifth full round set for the 23rd and 24th. The statement frames the goal as a comprehensive agreement and lists the working architecture: the pilot zones from which the IDF is to withdraw and into which the Lebanese Armed Forces are to deploy, the disarmament of Hezbollah inside those zones, and the move-for-move concession sequence first articulated in the fourth round of June 2 and 3. The talks resume four days after the Bekaa Valley exchanges and five days after the Versailles signature of the United States-Iran Memorandum of Understanding. The Stratagem reads the Litani Tender as the move at which the trilateral converts the post-Hormuz configuration into an institutional disarmament instrument — Mearsheimer on Iran's umbrella having been priced out of the regional architecture, Voss on the tactical-empathy concession sequence inside which the pilot zones operate, Greene's thirteenth law on appealing to each cohort's self-interest rather than to mercy or principle, and the opening of the calendar against which Hezbollah's residual refusal posture will be tested.

From the Desk of Statecraft·Filed Naqoura & Washington·22 June 2026

Naqoura —The statement is short and its language is procedural. The United States, Lebanon and Israel confirm that the political and security tracks will reconvene this week; the fifth full round will sit on the 23rd and 24th; the working agenda is the pilot zones, the move-for-move sequence, and the comprehensive agreement. There is nothing in the text that did not exist after Washington in early June; nothing that names Hezbollah; nothing that commits a date for IDF withdrawal from the five hilltops in the south. What has changed sits outside the statement. The Versailles signature on the United States-Iran Memorandum of Understanding was deposited five days before the joint statement. The Bekaa exchanges of Friday the 19th were the last serious kinetic operation of the previous configuration. The trilateral now resumes inside a regional architecture in which the Iranian umbrella over Hezbollah has been priced out by a deal it cannot reopen without losing the Hormuz concession the deal delivered.

Begin with the structural reading. Mearsheimer's reading of the regional power's revealed preference holds at its hardest register here: Hezbollah is a forward instrument of an Iranian deterrent that no longer needs the forward instrument in the configuration the Hormuz MoU has installed. Tehran's strategic priority across the next sixty days is to make the MoU work — the sanctions relief, the unblocked funds, the negotiated nuclear-program closure — not to subsidise the standing Hezbollah refusal posture that complicates every one of those files. The Iranian Foreign Ministry has already named the termination of the war on all fronts, including Lebanon, as among the points discussed. The Lebanese state's own structural position is more straightforward: a government under President Aoun and Prime Minister Salam has, across the past year, declared the intent to recover the monopoly of force and approved plans to disarm Hezbollah; the constraint was never the political will but the cost of moving against the militia without an external instrument to hold the gun on its back. The trilateral is the external instrument. Israel's structural position is symmetric — the IDF can withdraw from the five hilltops at acceptable risk once the Lebanese Armed Forces can credibly hold them, and not before. The pilot zones are the architecture inside which that credibility is being constructed at low political cost to each side.

Then read Voss on the calibrated tactical-empathy concession sequence. The negotiation craft that arrives at agreement by making each move-for-move concession feel to the counterparty like the concession that solves his own problem reads the pilot-zone mechanism at its operational form. Each pilot zone is small enough that an Israeli withdrawal is reversible; each LAF deployment is large enough that the Hezbollah loss of position is visible to the southern Shia constituency; each disarmament step inside the zone is bounded enough that the militia's central authority is not asked to acknowledge the full architectural defeat at any single negotiating session. The fourth-round Washington architecture was the breakthrough at the technique register: the parties stopped negotiating an end-state and started negotiating a sequence whose accumulation is the end-state. The fifth round on the 23rd and 24th is where the sequence either compounds into the second and third pilot zones or stalls inside the first one. Greene's thirteenth law on appealing to self-interest, not mercy or gratitude, is the trilateral's revealed discipline: nobody at the table is being asked to do anything for the counterparty; each side is being shown the move that advances its own structural position.

What the talks install.

The pilot zones are an instrument with two intended audiences and one unintended one. The intended audiences are the Israeli northern-security cohort, which needs the LAF deployment to be visible before the IDF withdrawal can be sustained politically inside the Knesset, and the Lebanese state-monopoly cohort, which needs the disarmament to be visible inside the southern Shia constituency before the broader Hezbollah arms can be moved against without civil-war risk. The unintended audience is Hezbollah's own leadership. The militia's structural problem is not the disarmament demand itself; it is the demand's compatibility with the Iranian funding cycle. As long as Tehran underwrote the arms, the militia's posture against the Lebanese state was politically free. With Tehran reordering its priorities against the MoU, the cost of the refusal posture is now paid by the militia's own constituency, in the kinetic exchanges and the southern reconstruction the refusal forecloses. Hezbollah rejected the early-June proposal as insufficient; the rejection was issued from a strategic configuration in which Iran could be relied upon to absorb the consequence. The June 22 round opens inside a configuration in which that assumption no longer holds.

What the southern hilltops test.

The test inside the next ninety days is not whether a comprehensive agreement is signed; it is whether the second and third pilot zones are established on the move-for-move calendar the fourth round set. Each successful zone compresses the militia's refusal posture inside a smaller territorial frame and raises the political cost of further refusal inside the southern Shia constituency. Each stalled zone restores the militia's position and raises the political cost on the Israeli and Lebanese state cohorts of having committed to a sequence whose returns the counterparty has stopped delivering. Confucius on the rectification of names reads the latent issue: until the IDF withdraws from a hilltop the LAF then holds, the joint statement is a procedural artefact; once the IDF withdraws and the LAF holds, the regional vocabulary moves from ceasefire to disarmament. The fifth round is therefore the round at which procedure converts into precedent or fails to. Talleyrand's discipline on the negotiator's posture — do not over-spend a concession that the counterparty has not yet asked for — reads the trilateral's craft: each side now holds back the concession that would unlock the next sequence, in order to be able to deliver it at the moment the deliverable arrives. The statement was procedural. The week is not.

The Bullion Tilt

The World Gold Council's 2026 Central Bank Gold Reserves Survey, published this month, reports that a record 95 per cent of reserve managers expect global official gold holdings to rise across the next twelve months and a record 43 per cent expect their own institution to add. Net central-bank purchases were 244 tonnes in the first quarter alone, against an annual average of 137 tonnes across the post-1990 sample. Gold has settled above four thousand three hundred dollars an ounce through the post-Hormuz disinflationary tail. The survey's headline is the threshold: at constant prices, gold's share of official reserves has now passed the dollar-denominated US Treasury share for the first time in the post-Bretton-Woods architecture. The Stratagem reads the Bullion Tilt as the move at which the global reserve cohort visibly converts a standing crisis-insurance preference into a structural rotation — Dalio on the Big-Cycle reserve-currency transition at its first publicly priced threshold, Lee Kuan Yew on the discipline of sovereign capital under a multipolar architecture, Munger on the central-bank incentive-caused bias the post-2022 sanctions regime installed, and the opening of the calendar inside which dollar-clearing risk acquires a publicly visible market form.

From the Desk of Capital·Filed London & Frankfurt·22 June 2026

London —The survey is the most boring instrument in the global monetary architecture. The World Gold Council polls about seventy reserve managers annually; the questionnaire is the same year after year; the published report runs along the same template. What changes is the answers. The 2026 print is the one in which the answers stopped being decorative. A record share of central banks expects the official cohort to add gold across the next year; a record share expects to add itself; the realised purchases through the first quarter are the fastest pace since 2013. The threshold the survey records is the one no central banker volunteers: gold's share of official reserves has, at constant prices, passed the dollar-denominated US Treasury share for the first time since 1944. The rotation is not new. The visibility of the rotation is.

Begin with Dalio. The Big-Cycle framework's observation that the late phase of a reserve-currency anchor is the phase in which the cohort of foreign holders moves from accumulating the currency to diversifying away from it, and that move is visible first in the official sector reads the WGC survey at its sharpest register. Across the post-2022 architecture — the Russian-reserves freeze, the SWIFT messaging exclusions, the long sanctions list, the secondary tariffs — the official cohort outside the G7 has been told, repeatedly and in plain English, that dollar-clearing access is conditional on alignment with US foreign policy and revocable at low notice. The rational response of any reserve manager whose sovereign cannot afford to be on the wrong side of a future enforcement decision is to convert the marginal reserve dollar into an asset that cannot be frozen by a single counterparty's decision. Gold is the only such asset at scale. The price moves up; the cohort buys more; the cohort's incentive structure compounds the rotation; the rotation lifts the price further; the next sovereign whose policy diverges from Washington's faces a higher cost of getting caught flat. The mechanism is recursive on the up-leg the way the dollar-accumulation mechanism was recursive on the up-leg between 1971 and 2008.

Then read Lee Kuan Yew. The Singapore-trained discipline that the sovereign that wishes to preserve strategic autonomy must hold its reserves in an asset whose value does not depend on the goodwill of any single foreign capital reads the bullion tilt at its political-economy form. The Chinese reserve cohort has been the loudest publicly disclosed buyer; the actual cohort is wider. The Saudi Arabian Monetary Authority, the National Bank of Kazakhstan, the Reserve Bank of India, the Central Bank of Turkey, and the Czech National Bank have all been buyers across the past two years. The shared characteristic is not ideology; it is structural exposure. Each holds a reserve portfolio whose value, in the post-2022 configuration, has a non-trivial probability of being interfered with by a US Treasury enforcement decision, and each has therefore been rebuilding its reserves toward the asset that cannot be interfered with. LKY's point is older than the dollar: a state that cannot defend its own money against another state's decisions is not strategically independent in any meaningful sense. The bullion tilt is the operational form of that proposition at the reserve-manager register.

What the rotation installs.

Munger on incentive-caused bias reads the central-bank governance register at its sharpest form. Reserve managers, like all bureaucrats, are paid more for not getting fired than for outperforming a benchmark. The benchmark the previous generation of reserve managers was paid against was the implicit US Treasury yield curve. The benchmark the present generation is being paid against is the survival of the sovereign's strategic autonomy across the next ten years. The first benchmark rewarded dollar weight; the second rewards diversification. The instrument that benefits is gold for the simple reason that it is the only large-stock reserve asset that does not have a sovereign issuer with the power to cancel it. The euro lacks the depth, the renminbi lacks the convertibility, the yen lacks the demographic spine; the basket alternatives carry the same political risk as the dollar in different proportions. The WGC's published reason ordering — performance during crisis at 90 per cent, long-term store of value at 84, portfolio diversification at 82 — is the polite version of the underlying answer. Crisis performance, in a reserve-manager's vocabulary, is the polite phrase for the unfreezeable asset.

What the threshold tests.

The threshold the survey has crossed is mechanical: at the marginal allocation cycle, central banks are now allocating more new reserve flow to gold than to US Treasuries, and the realised price effect is compounding into a stock-level inversion. The structural test inside the next ninety days is how the US Treasury market absorbs the loss of marginal sovereign demand at the long end of the curve. The ten-year settled at 4.46 per cent through the post-FOMC week; the term premium has been widening across the first half of 2026; the realised dollar coverage at the thirty-year auction sequence has been thinner. Confucius on the rectification of names reads the latent issue at its broader register: the asset that has been called the global risk-free rate for forty years is now being priced by its largest historical sovereign buyer as a risk-bearing asset. The vocabulary will eventually adjust to the pricing. Mearsheimer's observation on the structural costs of hegemonic decline applies at its money-architecture form: the privilege of the reserve issuer was that everyone wanted to hold the issuer's debt; the cost of the privilege's erosion is that the issuer has to pay more to finance the same debt against a smaller bid. The peace premium the Hormuz MoU has just delivered has bought the Federal Reserve some time on the energy-shock inflation file. The bullion tilt suggests it has not bought the dollar-Treasury complex any time at all.

The Practitioner's Note

The interest behind the position.

Each of Monday's stories turns on the same instrument: a principal identifies the underlying interest behind the counterparty's stated position and designs the concession that addresses that interest at low cost to the principal's own position. Witkoff and the trilateral identify the Hezbollah refusal posture as not an irreducible ideological commitment but a function of the Iranian funding cycle, and design the pilot-zone sequence inside the new post-MoU configuration. The reserve-manager cohort identifies the post-2022 sanctions architecture as having rewritten the self-interest equation and rotates the marginal reserve dollar accordingly. The Cypriot Presidency identifies Budapest's veto as a specific Hungarian-minority concern and brokers the bilateral settlement that converts the procedural blockage into a political return. In each case the operational discipline is identical: do not negotiate against the stated position; identify the underlying interest the position protects, and design the move that addresses that interest at low cost to your own.

In your own affairs: the law cuts at three registers. At the professional register, when a counterparty refuses your request, ask which underlying interest the refusal protects and which alternative move would address that interest without forcing you to surrender the underlying need on your side. The standoff is almost always at the level of the stated position; the resolution is almost always at the level of the underlying interest. At the personal register, when a relationship deteriorates, ask which interest of the counterparty you have failed to acknowledge across the recent cycle, and which concrete move on your side would convert acknowledgement into resolution. At the negotiating register, when you find yourself appealing to the counterparty’s mercy, gratitude, or sense of fairness, you have already lost the negotiation; the appeal is the symptom of having failed to find the interest-aligned move.

The hard question: the law has a second edge. The appeal to self-interest is durable only when the underlying interest is real and specifically addressed; the appeal to a fabricated interest, or to an interest the counterparty does not actually hold, is brittle and exposed at the first test. Before you frame a request as the counterparty’s self-interest, ask: have I correctly identified the interest, or am I projecting an interest the counterparty does not in fact hold? The trilateral works because the Iranian repricing is real; the gold rotation works because the sanctions architecture is real; the Hungarian unlock worked because the Transcarpathia file is real. The discipline is twofold: identify the interest with the precision the counterparty himself would recognise, and design the move that addresses it at a cost you can afford to bear if the deal compounds.

Apply the test to your own week — where in your work, your negotiations, or your relationships are you arguing against the stated position rather than addressing the underlying interest? Which counterparty have you been asking for a favour when you could have been showing them the move that advances their own interest? Identify the interest with the precision the counterparty himself would recognise, design the move that addresses it at a cost you can afford, and verify that the interest is real before you frame the appeal.
