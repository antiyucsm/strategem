The Nyala Market

A Sudanese Armed Forces drone struck a fuel market on the outskirts of Nyala on Tuesday afternoon, May 19. Local committees and the RSF-aligned Sudan Founding Coalition reported at least ten civilians killed, dozens wounded, and the death of one Coalition official, Osama Hassan. The strike is the third consecutive day of SAF air operations against the RSF de facto capital in South Darfur, and the second strike inside a week to land on a civilian market rather than a fortified position. It sits inside a broader documentary cycle. On May 4, Sudan's Foreign Ministry in Port Sudan publicly accused the United Arab Emirates and Ethiopia of orchestrating the drone strikes that hit Khartoum International Airport and Port Sudan in the first days of the month. The Conflict Insights Group's April 22 phone-tracking investigation, picked up by the Financial Times and Al Jazeera, documented a Colombian-mercenary pipeline running from a UAE military training facility at Ghayathi in Abu Dhabi to the RSF anti-aircraft and drone-defence operations centred on Nyala — the Wi-Fi network names “ANTIAEREO” and “AirDefense” logged by the tracked phones in the RSF capital are the documentary thread. The US State Department on April 15, on the third anniversary of the war, called for an immediate ninety-day humanitarian truce; on May 8, Al Jazeera's three-year retrospective named the conflict a “war overshadowed.” The Stratagem reads the Nyala strike as one frame in a regional proxy architecture that the Iran-war headlines have institutionally crowded off the page — the same UAE that was hit at Barakah on May 17 is the documentary sponsor of the RSF drone operation at Nyala the SAF is now striking, and the symmetry is the story.
