The Polish Scramble

A pair of Polish F-16s climbed into the night over the country’s southeastern provinces on Saturday and held station for just under four hours while Russian long-range aviation worked through a multi-wave cruise missile and drone bombardment across western Ukraine. A NATO airborne early-warning aircraft circled with them. By daybreak Sunday the Operational Command stood the pairs down: no Polish airspace had been violated this cycle. The scramble looks tactical and is partly tactical. It is also the documentary cadence of something larger. The eastern flank of the alliance has, since last autumn, absorbed Russian drones into Romanian airspace near the Danube, repeat penetrations of Estonian airspace, and an incident pattern Brussels now refers to in working papers as the gray-zone air theatre — an architecture in which the threshold of an Article 5 trigger is institutionally tested without ever being crossed.
