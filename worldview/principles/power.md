# Power

## Definition

> Power is the ability to get others to do as you wish.

Everything else is derivative.

## Levers of influence

Human behavior moves along predictable incentives. Every strategic action activates or withholds one of these:

- **Money** — financial gain, wealth, profit
- **Rivalry** — outperforming a competitor, eliminating a threat
- **Knowledge** — information, expertise, insider access
- **Pleasure** — enjoyment, comfort, luxury
- **Recognition** — praise, acknowledgment, status reward
- **Relationships** — alliance, connection, social capital
- **Growth** — personal development, skill, mastery
- **Reputation** — long-term standing, prestige, legacy
- **Security** — stability, safety, risk reduction
- **Morality** — alignment with values, moral satisfaction
- **Impact** — significance, the mark left behind

When an actor's motive is unclear, run the list. One fits.

## The four questions of combat

Every conflict resolves against four asymmetries:

1. Who can **initiate** combat on favorable terms?
2. Who can **disengage** cleanly when position turns against them?
3. Who can **end** the conflict on their own terms?
4. Who can **endure** longer than the opponent?

Power accrues to whoever answers these more confidently.

## On submission

Submitting in spirit to an aggressor does not buy peace. It buys an exploitative relationship. Aggressors push until stopped. Peace arrives only when resistance is credible.

## Corollaries for reading the world

- Power exists in **concentrated forms**. Diffused power is absence.
- **Visible** power is weaker than **unseen** power. Announced plans invite resistance.
- **Dependence** is the best lock. Voluntary dependence is stronger than coerced dependence, because the subject mistakes it for choice.
- **Incentives are superresponsive.** Rewards and punishments shape behavior before values do — often more than values do.
- **Concentration has a twin edge.** Tied to a single source of power, you fall with it.

## What this principle refuses

- *"Some have power, some don't."* Everyone has levers. The question is which ones are active in a given moment.
- *"Power corrupts."* Power **reveals**. Corruption was already there; concentration exposes it.
- *"X is good, Y is bad."* Labels are downstream of interests. Ask whose interests first.
